A blue heeler, or Australian cattle dog, is a breed of herding dog originally developed in Australia for droving cattle over long distances across rough terrain. This breed is a medium-sized, short-coated dog that occurs in two main color forms. A blue heelers life span is typically 13 to 15 years.

Blue heelers are solid, sturdy and compact dogs with an alert, ready to work stance. They are slightly longer than they are tall with curved, hanging tails, they have muscular legs and strong necks with broad somewhat rounded heads and pointy ears.
